Performance
Why ML Over Simple Heuristics?

This is a predictive model that extrapolates marketing spend for companies that don't publicly disclose it. It is not ground truth — it's designed for relative comparisons, ranking, and benchmarking.

The key question: does the ML model capture any real signal, or would a simple heuristic (e.g. "predict the industry median") work just as well?

Heuristic baselines contain almost no predictive signal

We systematically tested every reasonable heuristic — predicting the global median, the sector median, sector+industry median, and sector+industry+customerType median. None exceeded R² = 0.10 in log-space. Most were near zero or negative. Even grouping by sector+industry — the most granular heuristic — explains at best 2–10% of variance.

The ML model, using 40 company features (financials, digital footprint, sector, funding stage), achieves R² of 0.27–0.73 — a 4–12x improvement over the best heuristic. This confirms the model is capturing real, multi-dimensional signal that simple groupings cannot.

How the Model Works

  • Four LightGBM regression models each predict a log-transformed target: total marketing expense, influencer spend, advertising spend, and sponsorship spend.
  • Hyperparameter tuning via Optuna (Bayesian optimization, 25 trials per target), with 5-fold cross-validation and early stopping.
  • All targets are log-transformed (log1p) because marketing spend spans orders of magnitude — $100K for a small startup vs. $10B for a global CPG brand.
  • The final top-down allocation predicts total marketing expense first, then distributes across channels using model weights blended with industry priors (70% prior / 30% model) to correct for measurement biases.

Channel Definitions

Digital Advertising Display & social ad spend (scaled to approximate total digital). Does not include TV, print, radio, or OOH.
Influencer Creator partnerships on Instagram & YouTube (scaled proxy).
Sponsorship Sponsorship deals (sports, events, entertainment).
Other / Residual Everything else: events, content marketing, PR, team costs, TV/print/radio/OOH, trade shows, etc. Computed as Total Marketing − tracked channels.

This is a predictive model that extrapolates spend for the full universe of companies — most of which never publicly disclose marketing budgets. Estimates are most reliable for relative ranking within an industry and cohort-level patterns. Individual company figures should be treated as order-of-magnitude predictions, not audited actuals.
